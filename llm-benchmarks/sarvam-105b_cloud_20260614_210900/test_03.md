### 2. Tanglish (Tamil-English Mix)

**Message:**
Anna, deployment pipeline aagide. Environment variable config la something wrong iruku pola. Pls check panni paathu solli vaanga. Fix panna enna panrom?

**Breakdown:**
*   **"Anna"**: A respectful and common way to address a senior or respected colleague, literally "older brother".
*   **"aagide"**: The Kannada/Tamil equivalent of "aag gaya hai" - it means "it has happened" or "it's done," often used to state a problem.
*   **"config la"**: "la" is the Tamil dative case marker, used here to mean "in" or "on".
*   **"something wrong iruku pola"**: "iruku" means "is there" and "pola" means "looks like". This is a very natural way to express a suspicion.
*   **"panni paathu solli vaanga"**: A polite but firm request meaning "please go check and tell me".
*   **"Fix panna enna panrom?"**: "enna panrom?" translates to "what should we do?" and is a standard way to ask for a solution.
